## 日本語訳

韓国の代表的なソフトウェア企業として注目を集めるQueryPieを率いるのは、Kakao出身の若手創業者であるHwang In-seo氏です。記事では、Salesforce Venturesや日本のZ Venture Capitalから投資を受け、海外展開を加速している同社の成長が取り上げられています。

Hwang氏は慶尚北道・浦項で育ち、小学生の頃からプログラミングを始め、2013年にはKakaoに若手開発者として参加しました。その後、仲間とともに独立し、QueryPieを創業しました。

記事によれば、QueryPieのデータ分析・セキュリティソリューションは、スタートアップ支援や海外ネットワークの獲得を追い風に勢いを増し、Yanoljaからのクラウドデータベース保護ニーズに応えて短期間でソリューションを完成させたことが、KakaoやLG U+などの顧客拡大につながったとされています。

また、QueryPieはクラウド世界の「ドアロック」や「監視カメラ」のような役割を果たすと表現されており、韓国で培われた高いセキュリティ規制対応力が海外でも競争力になっていると説明されています。Hwang氏は、日本の大手顧客獲得に向けた検証や、英国・スペインを含む欧州でのPoCを進めており、AI機能を取り入れたセキュリティソリューションも年内後半の投入を計画していると述べています。

**Original title:** Advancing into Japan and Europe with a Security Solution Serving as the “Door Lock” of the Cloud World

A cloud-based data protection solution startup founded by the youngest developer-turned-founder of Kakao is representing Korea's software country. Hwang In-seo (photo), CEO of Quarrypie, a Korean security software company that became a hot topic after being selected as a representative accelerator in the U.S. and invested in the U.S. Salesforce and Japan's Jet Venture Capital (ZVC), is the main character.

Born and raised in rural Pohang, North Gyeongsang Province, Hwang started programming when he was in the second grade of elementary school and dominated the Information Olympiad. From the age of 19, he joined Kakao, the fifth company, as the youngest developer in 2013 after working as a mobile application company and a kiosk manufacturing company for special military service. "Giving Kakao Talk gifts" is his work. Then, he left the company with his like-minded colleagues and founded it in 2016.

It is a self-developed data analysis solution that was immediately selected by the Accelerator Wy Combinator, which Sam Altman represented at the time. Thanks to this, it also gained a U.S. corporate network and accumulated investment of 40 billion won from overseas venture capital (VC). When I was staying in Korea, I heard a complaint from a Yanolja official that "there is no solution to support cloud database security" and went to work as Yanolja and completed a related solution in six months. As word of mouth went viral, Kakao and LG U+ became customers one after another.

In the cloud world, query-Fi acts as a door lock or a closed-circuit (CC) TV.

South Korea's strong security regulations and compliance levels have become more competitive overseas. CEO Hwang said, "We are conducting tests through partner companies to penetrate large Japanese customers and have begun testing technology verification (PoC) in Europe such as the UK and Spain. We will also launch security solutions that incorporate artificial intelligence (AI) functions in the second half of the year after stability tests."
